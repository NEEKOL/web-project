    Tom Yam Curry

    This spicy curry originating from South East Asia is packed with flavours,nutrition and is a simple way to use up leftover vegetables at the end of the week. While preparing this dish traditionally would take a lot of ingredients and time, most supermarkets nowadays in the UK offer tom yam paste, making it a hassle free recipe

    Ingredients

    • Tom yam curry paste
    • Cleaned prawns or minced chicken (vegan substitute: Tofu or mushrooms)
    • Coconut milk
    • Sliced vegetables (Most vegetables you have will work. Suggested: cabbage, cauliflower or thinly sliced carrots. Avoid: courgettes, pumpkin or squash)
    • Rice noodles

    Method

    1. Lightly cook the prawns or minced chicken in a little oil with the tom yam curry paste
    2. Add the coconut milk, and some hot water if necessary
    3. Add the vegetables.
    4. Add the rice noodles a minute or so after the vegtables, as they will take less time to soften.

    Creamy brocolli pasta

    This simple recipe is typical in Italian homes. The ease of preparation and minimal ingredients make a surprisingly nutritious and delicious meal.

    Ingredients

    • Uncooked pasta
    • Brocolli
    • Olive oil
    • Salt
    • Optional: Cheese

    Method

    1. Chop the brocolli roughly. It isn't necessary to be very precise as they will eventually crumble
    2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, then add the pasta and brocolli florets. It is essential to cook them together to achieve a creamy consistency at the end.
    3. Once the pasta is cooked, drain the water. Add olive oil and salt to the mixture, and if you wish to, grate in some cheese.

    Teriyaki Salmon

    The interesting flavours in this East Asian recipe are bound to satisfy.

    Ingredients

    • Salmon fillet (vegan substitute: Tempeh)
    • Bell pepper, mushrooms, white onion, or courgette

    For the teriyaki sauce

    • Minced garlic
    • Soy sauce
    • Sesame oil
    • Balsamic vinegar (optional)
    • Honey
    • Black pepper
    • Salt

    Method

    1. Chop your vegetables of choice and set aside for later
    2. Pan-fry the salmon fillets skin-down for 9 minutes, flipping over halfway
    3. Add the chopped vegetables into the pan around 3 minutes before the salmon has cooked

    Homemade Noodles

    A healthier homemade take on instant noodles; just as convenient for the busy student without processed ingredients. Boil it with vegetables of your choice to increase the nutritional content if desired.

    Ingredients

    • Egg noodles
    • Soy sauce
    • Sesame oil
    • Eggs (2 or 3)
    • Chilli powder

    Method

    1. Boil some water and cook the egg noodles in a pot.
    2. Shortly before the noodles are cooked, crack in the eggs one by one.
    3. Drain the contents carefully to avoid breaking the poached eggs.
    4. Add a dash of soy sauce, some sesame oil, and chilli according to taste to season the noodles before serving.

    Risotto

    A risotto packs nutrients, is easy to prepare and makes a hearty dish during the colder seasons. Here we have a recipe for a mushroom risotto, but the idea can be easily adapted for variations using different combinations of stock/soup base, protein and vegetables.

    Ingredients

    • Chestnut mushrooms
    • Arborio rice
    • Vegetable stock
    • Garlic
    • Parsley (fresh or dried herb form)
    • 50g of butter
    • Parmesan cheese

    Method

    1. Slice the mushrooms and mince the garlic.
    2. Lightly fry the arborio rice and garlic until golden.
    3. Add the mushrooms into the pot. Then, add the vegetable stock and hot water.
    4. Bring the mixture to a boil on medium-high heat, then decrease the heat. Avoid stirring at all until the rice is cooked, to prevent the bottom from sticking to the pot.
    5. Add a little chopped parsley, stir in the parsley and grate in some parmesan cheese to finish before serving.
